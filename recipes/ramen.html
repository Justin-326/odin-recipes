Ramen Recipe

Description

Tonkotsu (豚骨, とんこつ) is a style of ramen noodle soup that originated in Fukuoka Prefecture on the island of Kyushu in western Japan. Tonkotsu ramen is famed for its intensely flavorful and meaty broth that’s derived from pork bones (ton means pork and kotsu means bones in Japanese).

Ingredients

  • Fresh ramen noodles
  • Pork leg bones
  • Pork hock with skin
  • Kombu
  • Iriko
  • Katsuobushi
  • Dried shiitake mushrooms
  • Garlic
  • Fresh Ginger
  • Onion
  • Scallions
  • Usukuchi
  • Rice vinegar
  • Sake
  • Mirin
  • Kosher salt
  • Water
  • Instant Pot
  • Toppings of your choice

Steps

  1. Rinse and soak the pork bones and pork hock in cold water.
  2. In the Instant Pot, bring the broth ingredients up to pressure (takes 40 minutes), then cook the tonkotsu broth ingredients under high pressure for 1 hour.
  3. Meanwhile, start the cold brew dashi (Japanese soup stock)
  4. Make the shoyu tare (soy sauce-flavored seasoning sauce).
  5. After pressure cooking, uncover the broth and boil over high heat (Sauté mode) for 30 minutes. Skim the scum and foam.
  6. Add the hidden seasoning ingredients and the dashi solid ingredients. Bring it to a rolling boil
  7. After 30 minutes, add the dashi liquid. Boil for 30 minutes more and stir to blend the broth components.
  8. Strain the tonkotsu ramen broth.
  9. Boil the ramen noodles to your desired doneness. Add the shoyu tare in individual bowls.
  10. Serve the noodles with hot broth. Add your toppings and serve immediately.
